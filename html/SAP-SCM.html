Supply Chain Management (SCM)

What is Supply Chain Management?

There are many definitions for what Supply Chain Management is...BUT it comes down to the same fundamental questions:

  • How is an organization going to move their product(s) from point A to point B?
  • How is an organization going to source and procure the materials they need so they can assemble their products?
  • What are the transportation methods involved?
Supply Chain Management (SCM) is a critical module in SAP S/4HANA. It consists of the network of retailers, distributors, transporters, plants, and suppliers who participate in the purchasing, production, storage, and transportation of a product.

Supply Chain Management is the management of the movement of materials, money and information from one place to another.

Supply Chain Management consists of the movement of a material / product from the point where it is produced all the way until it is consumed. This includes all of the processes that transform raw materials into final products.

What are the subprocesses within Supply Chain Management (SCM)

  • Transportation Management (TM)
  • Warehouse Management (WM)
  • Extended Warehouse Management (EWM)
